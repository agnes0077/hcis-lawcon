About the Event

Where Students Discover
the Power of Law

HCIS LAWCON is a student-led legal conference that immerses young minds in the world of law — through mock trials, expert panels, and direct mentorship from practising lawyers. Designed for students who are curious, articulate, and ready to argue.

"Future Lawyers is proud to support HCIS LAWCON — equipping the next generation with the legal literacy and advocacy skills our society needs."

— Future Lawyers

Programme

Day at a Glance

9:00 am
Registration Opens
Doors open. Collect your materials, find your seat, and get ready for the day ahead.
9:00 – 9:30
Registration
Complete check-in at the welcome desk. Please arrive on time to ensure a prompt start.
9:30 – 10:15
Opening Ceremony & GOH Speech
Official welcome from the organising committee, followed by the Guest of Honour's keynote address on law, advocacy, and the importance of legal literacy for young people.
10:15 – 10:30
Q&A Session
An open Q&A with our Guest of Honour. Participants are encouraged to ask questions about the legal profession and what it takes to become an advocate.
10:30 – 10:45
Movement to Court Rooms
Teams make their way to their assigned courtroom. Marshals will guide participants to the correct venue.
10:45 – 12:15
Court Room Session 1
The first round of mock trials begins. Format: 1 courtroom, 5 vs 5. Both teams present their case, examine witnesses, and make closing arguments before a bench of practising lawyers.
12:15 – 1:30
Lunch Break
Catered lunch is provided. Use this time to debrief with your team, regroup, and prepare for the afternoon session.
1:30 – 2:30
Court Room Session 2
The second round of mock trials. Teams switch roles and continue building their advocacy under the watch of our adjudicator panel.
2:30 – 3:00
Tea Break
A short refreshment break before the final segment of the day.
3:00 – 3:15
Team Feedback from Judges
Judges deliver direct, structured feedback to each team — covering argumentation, courtroom presence, cross-examination technique, and overall advocacy.
3:15 – 4:00
Movement to Student Hub
All participants and guests make their way to the Student Hub for the closing ceremony.
4:00 – 5:00
Closing Ceremony & Prize Giving
Awards are presented, closing remarks delivered, and the day is celebrated with a group photo.

Preparation

Academic Resources

Familiarise yourself with these materials before the conference to get the most out of your experience.

Introduction to Family Law
Family law governs legal relationships within families — covering marriage, divorce, child custody, and inheritance. In Singapore, key statutes include the Women's Charter and the Administration of Muslim Law Act. You do not need to be an expert; a basic understanding of these principles will help you engage confidently in the mock trial and courtroom sessions at LAWCON.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can attend HCIS LAWCON 2026?+
Any student aged 13 or older — including those turning 13 in the 2026 calendar year — is eligible. There is no maximum per school, so teams of any size are welcome.
Do I need prior legal knowledge?+
No prior legal knowledge is required. LAWCON is designed to introduce students to legal thinking and advocacy. Prep materials and readings will be provided in advance.
What is the registration deadline?+
Normal Registration closes 20 July 2026. Extended Registration closes 30 July 2026. Register early to receive pre-event materials promptly.
How much does it cost?+
Registration fees are to be confirmed and will be published before the form goes live. Payment is collected via a QR code included in the registration form.
What should I bring on the day?+
Bring your student ID, any pre-read materials, a notebook and pen, and your registration confirmation. Case files will be provided on the day. Smart casual or business casual attire is recommended.
Will meals be provided?+
Lunch will be provided as part of the conference. Dietary requirements will be collected via the registration form.
When will the full programme be released?+
Full programme details will be confirmed after our upcoming meeting with the partner law firm. Mass emails go out from mid-June 2026. All registered participants will be notified directly.
