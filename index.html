About the Event

Where Students Debate the Principles of Law

The HCIS Lawcon 2026 is a student-led moot that tests one's ability to engage, challenge and argue on the principles of law via the channels of cross-examinations of witnesses in establishing the facts before applying them to the statutes of law.

Participants take on the role of lawyers for the plaintiff or the defence to present arguments before a judge. In this moot, participants will examine a custody dispute of a child, guided by the key principles from the Women's Charter 1961.

The HCIS Lawcon 2026 offers students a valuable opportunity to develop essential skills such as critical thinking, persuasive communication, and structured argumentation. Participants will learn how to analyse factual scenarios, apply legal principles, and present their case clearly and confidently. They will also be guided by our partner in this conference, Future Lawyers Singapore, and have access to online resources and lectures prior to the release of the case problem.

Mooting provides a meaningful introduction to the study and practice of law. It allows students to experience how legal reasoning is applied in real-life situations while building confidence in public speaking. These skills are not only important for aspiring lawyers but are also highly relevant across a wide range of academic and future career pathways.

Our Partner
Future Lawyers Singapore
Future Lawyers Singapore (FLS) is the Singapore chapter of Future Lawyers International, a youth-led non-profit organisation dedicated to educating, empowering, and inspiring youths to consider a pathway in the legal field.
To date, FLS has partnered with various organisations including Rajah and Tann LLP, Pro Bono SG and the law faculty of the Singapore Management University. Thus far, FLS has engaged more than 1,800 youths to demystify the legal world.
Presently in partnership with Hwa Chong International School, the Mooting Department at FLS serves as an academic advisor and trainer, buttressing the academic quality of the mock trial conference and ensuring that participants have a fruitful learning experience.

Family Law
Family law in Singapore governs legal issues arising from family relationships, including marriage, divorce, maintenance, and the welfare of children. Specific to the moot for this conference, family law regulates how financial and care-giving responsibilities are allocated when relationships break down. The Women's Charter (1961) is the principal statute governing marriage, divorce, division of matrimonial assets, spousal and child maintenance. The Charter aims to safeguard vulnerable family members and promote fairness within the family unit.
The Case Problem
What do you do when both parents vie for sole custody of a child? Does the child have any say in what he/she wants for their life? And is there one party who is more worthy than the other? At a time when courts move to restore and heal a family already hurting from upheaval, what is in the best interest and welfare of the child. You will have to decide as you examine the Women's Charter.
Skills to Practise
  • Constructing a logical legal argument with evidence
  • Cross-examination — asking precise, purposeful questions
  • Active listening and responding under pressure
  • Clear, confident oral advocacy and public speaking
  • Reading and summarising legal documents quickly

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can attend HCIS LAWCON 2026?+
Any student aged 13 or older — including those turning 13 in the 2026 calendar year — is eligible. There is no maximum number of participants per school, so teams of any size are welcome.
Do I need prior legal knowledge?+
No prior legal knowledge is required. LAWCON is designed to introduce students to legal thinking and advocacy. Prep materials and readings will be provided in advance.
What should I bring on the day?+
Bring your student ID, any pre-read materials, a notebook and pen, and your registration confirmation. Case files will be provided on the day. Smart casual or business casual attire is recommended.
Will meals be provided?+
Lunch will be provided as part of the conference. Dietary requirements will be collected via the registration form.
